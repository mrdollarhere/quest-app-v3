# Question Types Explained

The platform supports 11 different interaction modules. Here is how they work:

### 1. Single Choice
Standard "pick one" question.
- **Example**: "What is the capital of France?" (A, B, C, D)

### 2. Multiple Choice
Students can select more than one answer.
- **Example**: "Select all prime numbers from the list below."

### 3. True / False
A simple yes/no or true/false toggle.

### 4. Multiple True / False
A list of statements where the student marks each one as True or False.
- **Example**: "Which of these facts about gravity are true?"

### 5. Short Text
Students type a word or phrase. It must match your answer exactly.
- **Example**: "What is the largest planet?" (Answer: Jupiter)

### 6. Dropdown
A list of options hidden inside a menu. Great for saving space.

### 7. Ordering
Students drag items up and down to put them in the right sequence.
- **Example**: "Arrange these events in chronological order."

### 8. Matching
Students drag answers from a "pool" and drop them onto their matching pair.
- **Example**: "Match the country to its flag."

### 9. Matrix Choice
A grid where students map items to categories.
- **Example**: "For each fruit, categorize it as Sweet, Sour, or Bitter."

### 10. Hotspot (Spatial)
Students click on specific parts of an image.
- **Example**: "Click on the engine in this car diagram."
- **Setup**: Use the "Zone Mapper" in the Admin panel to draw boxes on your image.

### 11. Rating
A simple 5-star scale. Used for feedback or self-evaluation.
